About

My journey with data has been an unexpected yet exciting ride. Coming from an engineering background, I never imagined diving so deep into the world of analytics. It all started when I attended a free workshop led by an ex-manager at Swiggy (India’s version of Uber Eats). That session opened my eyes to how data can drive business decisions, and I was hooked. Before that, I had worked as a Data Science & Research intern, but the business impact of data truly clicked for me during that workshop. Later, as a Programmer Analyst at Cognizant, I was responsible for ensuring real-time data updates on the company’s website. That experience reinforced my decision to pursue a Master’s in Business Analytics—an opportunity I was fortunate to receive with a full state government scholarship. Throughout my master’s journey at Boston University, I’ve explored data from every angle—whether through SQL, Python, cloud computing, Excel, or visualization tools like Tableau, Looker, and Power BI. I’ve also built and optimized ETL pipelines using Prefect and GCP, streamlining data workflows for better automation and efficiency. My curiosity led me to dive into machine learning and generative AI, where I’ve worked with LLM models to extract insights from unstructured data, automate reporting, and enhance decision-making processes. From developing sentiment analysis models to leveraging tools like OpenAI’s APIs for predictive analytics, I’ve been passionate about integrating AI-driven solutions into business intelligence.

What started as curiosity has now become my passion: uncovering insights, optimizing processes, and making data-driven decisions that create real-world impact.
